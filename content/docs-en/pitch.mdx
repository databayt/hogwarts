## What Hogwarts is

Hogwarts is an open-source school operating system. We automate the operational core of schools — admissions, classrooms, attendance, grading, fees, messaging — so educators spend less time on paperwork and more time on teaching.

- Live at [ed.databayt.org](https://ed.databayt.org)
- Public demo at [demo.databayt.org](https://demo.databayt.org)
- First paying-pilot live at [kingfahad.databayt.org](https://kingfahad.databayt.org) (King Fahad Schools, Sudan)
- Open source under SSPL-1.0 — [github.com/databayt/hogwarts](https://github.com/databayt/hogwarts)
- Arabic-first / RTL-native, English parity (2,247 / 2,243 strings)
- Multi-tenant subdomain isolation
- 4,912 TypeScript / TSX source files, 52 automated tests, 8 GitHub Actions workflows

For the canonical traction number set, see [Traction](/docs/traction).

## Investor pitch

For VC, angel, and family-office investors.

**Stage**: pre-seed bridge. **Target raise**: $250K-$500K across instruments (grant + SAFE-cap + accelerator stipend blended). **Instrument preference**: SAFE (cap, post-money). **Valuation cap stance**: $4M-$6M post-money.

**Why now**: live paying pilot, AR/EN parity, Sudan school reopening, Saudi Vision 2030 EdTech investment surge, AI-cost-reduction window.

**Why this**: only Arabic-first, open-source, multi-tenant K-12 SaaS at this maturity in MENA. Competitor matrix in [Competitors](/docs/competitors).

**Why us**: Sudanese-founded team distributed across Sudan/KSA/Egypt/Europe; 18 months of build evidence (14+ epics shipped); contributor model documented in [Shared Economy](/docs/shared-economy).

**Use of funds** (12-month, $500K scenario):

| Bucket                              | %   | $     |
| ----------------------------------- | --- | ----- |
| Engineering (1 senior hire)         | 35% | $175K |
| MENA sales (1 lead + 1 SDR)         | 30% | $150K |
| Pilot subsidies (free for 6 months) | 15% | $75K  |

**Deal terms we offer / refuse**: full term-sheet stance in [Investors](/docs/investors). Short version: SAFE preferred, 1× non-participating liquidation preference, no full ratchet, no board control at seed, info rights + pro-rata standard.

## Grant pitch

For multilateral, foundation, government, NGO grant officers.

We position under three angles. Choose the one that matches your mission.

### Angle A — Post-conflict Sudan reconstruction

17M Sudanese children out of school. Khartoum reopened schools February 2026 after a three-year hiatus. The UNESCO Transitional Education Plan 2025-27 is $580M with no scalable digital infrastructure. Hogwarts is the open-source school OS already deployed at [kingfahad.databayt.org](https://kingfahad.databayt.org), built by a Sudanese-founded team, Arabic-first, multi-tenant. We are not theoretical; we are operationally ready.

**Best fits**: UNESCO TEP, GIZ, USAID DIV, Mastercard Foundation, Open Society, UK FCDO, OPEC Fund, Arab Fund for Economic and Social Development.

### Angle B — Arabic-first digital public infrastructure

MENA serves 80M+ K-12 students. Incumbent platforms are bolt-on Arabic translations of English software. Hogwarts is **built RTL-native from day one**, with 2,247 Arabic-keyed strings at parity with 2,243 English. We are the open-source baseline that any government or ministry can self-host without vendor lock-in. Vision 2030 + NTDP have both education and AI infrastructure as named priorities.

**Best fits**: NTDP, RDIA SIGP, Monsha'at, KAUST, Wa'ed Ventures (Aramco), MCIT, SDAIA, Islamic Development Bank, Saudi Awqaf.

### Angle C — Open-source education commons

Education software is a public good held privately. Hogwarts is licensed under SSPL-1.0 — self-host free, commercial resale forbidden. The codebase is auditable; contributions are tracked via a documented contributor model (Mudaraba / Shirkat al-A'mal / Waqf-grounded). Every grant dollar that funds Hogwarts development funds an asset that the world can use.

**Best fits**: Mozilla Open Source Awards, Sovereign Tech Fund, GitHub Accelerator, IDRC, Patrick J. McGovern Foundation, Open Source Pledge programs.

| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |

## Why open source

Student data is sensitive and curriculum changes constantly — schools deserve auditable, extensible code, and contributors deserve transparent attribution. SSPL-1.0 keeps the code free for self-hosters and protects against parasitic SaaS resale.

The contributor economic model — Mudaraba / Shirkat al-A'mal / Waqf-grounded — is documented in [Shared Economy](/docs/shared-economy). It is also a feature funders care about: every grant or capital dollar flows partly to contributors via a transparent point system.
