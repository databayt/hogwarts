This doc is the **academic-partnership inventory**. Universities and training centers are a non-obvious but high-leverage capital + talent + grant channel for an open-source EdTech that runs an explicit contributor model. Adjacent to [Partners](/docs/partners) and [Sponsors](/docs/sponsors) but distinct: capital here is mostly in-kind (student labor, faculty endorsements, research grants where we are co-PI).

_Last verified: 2026-05-18._

## Executive summary

We have an unusual structural fit with universities. Three reasons:

1. **Open-source codebase** — students can read and contribute to a production codebase without breaking NDA or IP barriers.
2. **Existing contributor model** — [Shared Economy](/docs/shared-economy) already provides point-based valuation, vesting, and recognition for outside contributors.
3. **Real-world problem space** — schools, attendance, exams, fees, multi-tenant SaaS, AR-first product, AI integrations. Plenty of capstone material.

Win-win pattern:

- **Student wins**: a real-world graduation project, a deployed feature, a public commit history, a reference letter, possible employment / equity via the contributor model.
- **Hogwarts wins**: feature shipped, content (sample data, documentation, translations), bug fixes, design polish — at zero direct cost.
- **University wins**: industry-relevant capstone, accreditation evidence, employer relationship.

## Three partnership tiers

| Tier | Engagement                                    | Time horizon | Capital form                                         |
| ---- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Capstone / graduation project (semester team) | 3-6 months   | In-kind student labor; published contribution points |
| 2    | Training-center cohort (apprenticeship)       | 3-12 weeks   | In-kind cohort labor; possible cohort sponsorship    |
| 3    | Faculty research grant (we as industry co-PI) | 1-3 years    | Cash co-funded grant via the university              |

We pursue all three. Tier 1 is easiest to start; Tier 3 has the biggest capital impact.

## Tier 1: Capstone / graduation projects

**How it works**: a 3-6 person student team takes a Hogwarts-defined problem as their graduation project. We provide: problem brief, codebase access, mentor (1 hour / week), end-of-semester review. They deliver: working feature, documentation, contribution points via [Shared Economy](/docs/shared-economy).

**Where to pull problems from**: open issues in [github.com/databayt/hogwarts](https://github.com/databayt/hogwarts) labeled `good-first-capstone` (to be added). Examples of capstone-sized features:

- AI tutor MVP for one subject
- Offline-mode improvements for low-connectivity schools
- New translation locale (e.g., Hausa, Swahili)
- WhatsApp Evolution API improvements
- Mobile-responsive view for a specific persona (e.g., parent on a cheap Android)
- Accessibility audit (WCAG 2.1 AA) of one feature block

**How to start**: write a 1-page memo to the department chair offering the partnership. Memo template:

```
Subject: Industry capstone proposal — Hogwarts (open-source EdTech)

Dear Prof. {{chair_name}},

databayt operates Hogwarts — an open-source, Arabic-first school operating system
deployed at {{schools_count}} schools across MENA, including {{recent_pilot}}.

We propose offering {{num_problems}} graduation-project problems for your {{semester}}
{{department}} students, each scoped for a team of 3-5. We provide problem briefs,
codebase access, a weekly mentor, and end-of-semester review. Students deliver working
contributions to a production codebase and receive contribution credit via our
documented contributor model.

Past departments that have engaged this model: {{references}}.

Could we set up a 30-minute call to discuss? Available {{availability}}.

Best,
{{name}}, databayt
{{email}} | ed.databayt.org
```

## Tier 2: Training-center cohorts

**How it works**: a training center (Tuwaiq Academy, Re:Coded, ALX, Misk Academy) places a cohort of 10-30 learners with us for 3-12 weeks. We provide: structured task list, mentorship, capstone evaluation. They provide: learners + cohort sponsorship (sometimes paid by the training center to us, sometimes by us to them).

**Best targets**:

- **Tuwaiq Academy (KSA)** — Vision 2030 bootcamp pipeline; pays placement fee to host industry. Fit: 5.
- **Re:Coded** — Sudan/Iraq/Turkey; refugee + underserved learners. Sponsor-supported. Fit: 5.
- **ALX (Pan-African)** — software engineering apprenticeship; their learners need real industry projects. Fit: 4.
- **Andela Learning Community** — Pan-African; large network. Fit: 4.

## Tier 3: Faculty research grant co-PI

**How it works**: a faculty member submits a research-grant application (to KAUST, KFUPM, IDRC, EU Horizon, etc.) with us as the industry co-PI. We bring the codebase, the data (anonymized), and the deployment. They bring the academic framing, the methodology, and the publication channel. Grant cash typically flows through the university with a budgeted industry-partner allocation.

**Best targets**:

- **KAUST faculty** — Innovation Fund + research grants. Strong AI / data / education research presence.
- **KFUPM faculty** — Strong CS / IS faculty; KFUPM Innovation Center; Saudi research grants flow through.
- **AUC faculty (Egypt)** — Strong applied CS + the Venture Lab adjacency.
- **University of Khartoum + SUST faculty** — Sudan-specific research grants via UNESCO, IDRC, AfricaConnect.

**How to find faculty**: identify CS/AI/Education faculty publishing in adjacent fields (school management systems, education AI, multi-tenant SaaS, AR-first UX). Cold-email with a specific 1-paragraph collaboration idea.

## Open-source contributor pipeline

Universities are our top-of-funnel for the open-source contributor pipeline. After capstone or cohort engagement, top students get invited to:

1. Continue contributing via [Shared Economy](/docs/shared-economy) point system.
2. Apply for paid contractor work as we hire.
3. Apply for full-time roles after graduation (with their contribution history as portfolio).

This is also how we build the advisor bench among faculty: a faculty member who supervised 2-3 successful capstones becomes a natural advisor candidate.

## Anti-patterns

- **Over-scoping a capstone**. A team of 3-5 students has ~200-400 hours over a semester. Scope features accordingly; don't ask for an entire feature block.
- **Treating capstone code as free production work**. Quality is uneven; we still need to review and refactor.
- **Skipping the contributor-credit acknowledgment**. Always credit students publicly on the [shared-economy](/docs/shared-economy) page and in commit history.
- **Vague faculty intros**. "We'd love to collaborate" is dead on arrival. Bring a specific 1-paragraph research idea.
