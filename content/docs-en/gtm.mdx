Hogwarts go-to-market is **one cohort, three phases**. Phase 1 lands ten free MENA pilots for feedback velocity. Phase 2 converts three of those to paid. Phase 3 scales paid acquisition. The motion is sequenced, not parallelized. This page is the one-page strategy; the doing happens in [Pilot](/docs/pilot), [Sales](/docs/sales), [Marketing](/docs/marketing), and [Leads](/docs/leads).

## One-line strategy

> Free 10-school MENA cohort → feedback → polish → trust → paid expansion.

We do not try to do paid acquisition while we still have one pilot. We do not try to scale to 100 schools while we are still polishing the product with 10. Each phase has an explicit gate.

## Phase 3 — Scale (Q3 2027 onward)

Only after Phase 2 closes. Operational themes (not milestones, because the metrics drive the timeline):

| Theme                                       | Trigger to start                                        |
| ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Paid LinkedIn / Google Arabic-keyword ads   | Phase 2 ad test profitable for 2 months                 |
| First sales rep hire (Arabic native)        | ≥$5K MRR sustained for 3 months                         |
| First government RFP response               | Government referral or open RFP that fits               |
| Reseller / partner channel                  | 2+ existing pilots ask for white-label / reseller terms |
| Regional expansion (French / Hindi locales) | MENA Year-2 retention ≥85%, 50+ paying schools          |

## Phase gate logic

We escalate to the next phase only when the gate is met. No exceptions. Skipping gates is the most common GTM failure mode — see [Principles](https://github.com/databayt/kun/blob/main/docs/PRINCIPLES.md#16-pick-a-beachhead-and-topple-it) (Geoffrey Moore — pick a bowling pin, topple it, then the next).

| If you find yourself …                                  | The phase gate signal is                 |
| ------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Doing paid ads with 2 pilots signed                     | Skipping Phase 1; stop                   |
| Hiring a sales rep at $500 MRR                          | Skipping Phase 2; stop                   |
| Pursuing government RFPs without any paid customer      | Vanity work; stop                        |
| Building a partner / reseller channel before pilots ask | Future work today; stop                  |
| 30 pilots signed but $0 MRR after 18 months             | Over-investing in breadth; stop, convert |

## Risks

| Risk                                                    | Likelihood | Impact   | Mitigation                                                      |
| ------------------------------------------------------- | ---------- | -------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Pilots churn before month 6                             | Medium     | High     | Weekly call anchor; engagement visible fast (see Pilot risks)   |
| Feedback overload — too many issues to triage           | Medium     | Medium   | Captain weekly review caps in-sprint scope                      |
| Single concentrated channel (e.g., Ali's Sudan network) | High       | Medium   | MENA-10 forces channel diversification across 6 countries       |
| No pilot converts to paid                               | Medium     | High     | Conversion conversation starts month 3, not month 6             |
| Skipping Phase 1 → Phase 3 under runway pressure        | High       | Critical | This document. Captain enforces phase gates.                    |
| Free pilot economics consume Abdout's runway            | Medium     | High     | Pilot infrastructure cost ≤ $40 / pilot / month (Vercel + Neon) |
| Competitor (Classera / Noon) signs MENA pilot at $0     | Low        | High     | Speed; we are MENA-built, they are not; we ship faster          |
| Sudan / MENA conflict disrupts pilot                    | Medium     | Variable | Geographic diversification, cloud-hosted, no on-prem dependency |
