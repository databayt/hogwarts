## Executive Summary

**Hogwarts** is a SaaS school management platform — 95% complete, live at [ed.databayt.org](https://ed.databayt.org). It automates enrollment, attendance, exams, timetables, grading, finance, and parent communication for K-12 schools. Arabic-first, RTL-native, multi-tenant architecture. Open source.

We are applying to two Sudanese incubator programs to accelerate market entry, starting with Sudan and expanding across MENA.

## The Opportunity

### Problem

Schools in Sudan and across MENA manage operations with paper registers, Excel sheets, and WhatsApp groups. Administrators lose 40+ hours/month to manual data entry. Student records are fragmented, attendance is unreliable, and parents are disconnected from their children's education.

### Solution

A complete cloud-based school operating system — one platform for enrollment, attendance, exams, timetables, grading, finance, and parent communication. Arabic-first, mobile-responsive, and priced for emerging markets.

### Traction

| Metric               | Status                                                               |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Product completeness | 95% — live at [ed.databayt.org](https://ed.databayt.org)             |
| Codebase             | 200K+ lines, 514 tests passing                                       |
| Architecture         | Multi-tenant (each school gets isolated subdomain)                   |
| Localization         | Arabic/English, full RTL support                                     |
| Stack                | Next.js 16, React 19, Prisma 6, TypeScript 5                         |
| Open source          | [github.com/databayt/hogwarts](https://github.com/databayt/hogwarts) |

### Business Model

Tiered SaaS subscription — Free (up to 100 students), Pro ($1.50/student/month), Enterprise ($1.00/student/month). See [Business Model](/docs/business-model) for full details.

### The Ask

Incubation support to secure first 5 pilot schools, mentorship on go-to-market in Sudan/MENA, and seed funding to sustain operations through first revenue.

## Orange Corners Sudan (9th Edition)

### Program Overview

| Detail    | Info                                                                    |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Organizer | Dutch Embassy via 249Startups                                           |
| Duration  | 6-month incubation program                                              |
| Funding   | EUR 5,000 seed + EUR 50,000 growth funding                              |
| Edition   | 9th (latest cohort)                                                     |
| Partners  | 249Startups, Haggar, El Nefeidi, Bank of Khartoum, UCB, CTC             |
| Website   | [249startups.com/orangecorners](https://249startups.com/orangecorners/) |

### Why We Fit

Orange Corners does not require a specific sector focus. We apply as what we are: a school automation SaaS company.

- **Product-market fit for Sudan**: Schools reopening post-conflict need management systems. We built exactly that.
- **Revenue model defined**: SaaS subscription with clear unit economics ($1-1.50/student/month). Not a charity project — a business.
- **Technical moat**: 200K+ lines of production code. Multi-tenant architecture means onboarding a new school takes minutes, not weeks.
- **Scalable across MENA**: Arabic-first platform works for any Arabic-speaking country. Sudan is the beachhead, MENA is the market.
- **Open source advantage**: Lower customer acquisition cost — schools can inspect the code, community contributes features, trust is built-in.
