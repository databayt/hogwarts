Hogwarts is an open-source, multi-tenant K-12 platform shipping in production at [ed.databayt.org](https://ed.databayt.org), with a live pilot at [kingfahad.databayt.org](https://kingfahad.databayt.org) (King Fahad Schools — founding member of the [MENA-10 free pilot cohort](/docs/pilot)). Where the established SIS vendors target large North-American districts with closed, English-first stacks, Hogwarts is Arabic-first, RTL-native, subdomain-isolated per school, and priced for emerging-market budgets. Browse the vendors below for inspiration; the feature comparison sets a baseline for parity and the [battle-card section](#why-mena-schools-choose-hogwarts) at the bottom is the prose for proposals and discovery calls.

## Differentiators

- **Arabic-first, RTL-native** — every screen designed for Arabic first, English second.
- **Open source** — schools can audit the code; community contributes; no vendor lock-in.
- **Multi-tenant by design** — `schoolId`-scoped queries, subdomain routing, complete data isolation per school.
- **Region-priced** — free tier up to 100 students; Pro $1.50/student/month; Enterprise $1.00/student/month.
- **MEA payment rails** — Stripe + bankak + tap + cash + bank transfer + mobile money.
- **AI-assisted admission** — document classification, merit ranking, bank-receipt extraction.

## Why MENA schools choose Hogwarts

Battle-card content for [Proposal](/docs/proposal) and [Sales](/docs/sales) discovery calls. Each row is one line a school admin should hear in 60 seconds, paired with the regional incumbent it counters.

| Concern                                      | What incumbents offer                                                  | What Hogwarts offers                                                            |
| -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Arabic UI quality                            | English UI translated, RTL bolted on (Classera, Noon, Blackboard MENA) | RTL is the default, every screen, every role; English is the translation        |
| Cost for a small private school              | $5K–$50K / year all-in (Classera, regional incumbents)                 | Free for ≤100 students; $1.50 / student / month thereafter                      |
| Data residency and audit                     | Closed binaries, opaque storage (most regional SaaS)                   | Open source, schools can self-host or audit; tenant data isolated by `schoolId` |
| Speed of onboarding                          | Weeks of setup; vendor implementation team                             | Subdomain in 24 hours; Day-0 to Day-30 founder-led onboarding                   |
| Roadmap influence                            | Closed roadmap, annual user-group conference                           | Weekly feedback call during pilot; co-design partner status at Year 1           |
| Vendor lock-in                               | Proprietary data formats, exit fees                                    | Full CSV / JSON export any time, SSPL-1.0 license                               |
| MENA payment rails                           | Stripe-only or none                                                    | Stripe + bankak (Sudan) + tap (Saudi) + cash + bank transfer + mobile money     |
| Post-conflict / emerging-market deployment   | Designed for large districts with fast internet                        | Schools coming online — mobile-responsive, low-bandwidth tolerant               |
| Time to first paying customer (from contact) | 6–12 months sales cycle                                                | 14-day trial → 6-month free pilot → 30%-off paid Year 1                         |
