The catalog is the platform-wide store of curriculum content. One copy of every subject, chapter, and lesson lives in shared tables with no `schoolId`; every school references that same content through small bridge rows instead of cloning it. Teacher- and school-contributed content (videos, materials, questions, books) flows through SaaS review and enriches the catalog for everyone.

## Content & assets

The catalog holds two kinds of things: **content** — the curriculum hierarchy (subjects → chapters → lessons) plus the exams, questions, materials, and assignments attached to it — and **assets** — the images, textbook PDFs, and videos that content references. Each is produced by a different seed or pipeline. The content pipeline runs source → seed → catalog tables → per-school bridge → school app:

<Mermaid
  chart={`flowchart LR
  Source["Authored source"] --> Seeds["Seeds"]
  Seeds --> Catalog[("Catalog tables")]
  Catalog -->|"setupCatalogForSchool"| Bridge["SubjectSelection"]
  Bridge --> School["School app"]
  School --> Student["Student LMS"]
`}
/>

Authored source — a JSON inventory, an on-disk tree, a CDN, or inline data — is pulled in by a **seed** that writes rows into the global `catalog_*` tables. `setupCatalogForSchool` then bridges a tenant to that shared content through `SubjectSelection` rows, and the school app reads through the bridge into classes, the timetable, and the LMS.

### Generated content — exams, questions, materials, assignments

`catalog/content.ts` reads the published hierarchy and **generates synthetic** rows in bulk:

- **Exams** — two per subject (`final`, `midterm`), two per chapter (`chapter_test`, `practice`), two per lesson (`quiz`, `diagnostic`), each linked to its scope and to questions through the `ExamQuestion` junction. Seeded `status: PUBLISHED` but left `approvalStatus: PENDING` — the one artifact not auto-approved.
- **Questions** — the catalog question bank: ~10 per subject, 6 per chapter, 4 per lesson, across eight question types, seeded `APPROVED` / `PUBLIC`. (Real teacher-authored questions live in the separate school-scoped `QuestionBank`, not here.)
- **Materials** — textbook/syllabus/reference per subject, study-guide/project per chapter, worksheet/slides/notes/lab-manual per lesson. **Metadata only — no real files are attached** (`fileUrl` is null).
- **Assignments** — one per lesson, so a curriculum without lessons gets none.

Because all of this reads the hierarchy, a subjects-only curriculum receives only the per-subject rows — no chapter- or lesson-scoped content, and no assignments. It is **placeholder data for populating the UI, not authored material**, and `catalog/content.ts` is not idempotent (re-running duplicates rows). The `Quiz` model is not seeded at all.

The exception is **exam scaffolding**: `catalog/exam-templates.ts` writes real, hand-authored **blueprints** (per subject category), two **exam templates** per subject, and global **paper templates** — genuine config, idempotent, covering every subject (they need no chapters).

### Images — thumbnails, banners, covers

All catalog imagery routes through the shared **concept** system and the Sharp pipeline in `image.ts`: each source image becomes four WebP variants (`sm` 200px, `md` 600px, `lg` 1200px, `original` lossless), uploaded to S3 and served via CloudFront; `image-url.ts` builds `{key}-{size}.webp` at read time. See [Image pipeline](#image-pipeline) and [Concepts](/docs/concept).

- **Thumbnails** — `catalog/concepts.ts` processes the 24 source images in `public/subjects/concepts/` into `catalog/concepts/g{grade}-{concept}/thumbnail-*.webp` for all 12 grades (~1,150 objects) and backfills each subject's `concept` and `thumbnail`. **Complete.**
- **Banners** — `catalog/banners.ts` copies pre-existing wide curated banners (raw, no Sharp) into the matching concept paths. Every subject gets a `banner` key, but the copy covers about ten concepts and silently skips any whose legacy source is gone — so some `banner` keys point at objects that don't exist. **Partial.**
- **Textbook covers** — every subject is given a `cover` key (`catalog/concepts/{concept}/cover`), but **no seed or script uploads an object to that path**, so the textbook card always falls back to an icon. **Missing.**

### Textbook PDFs

Sudan ships 118 `textbook.pdf` files under `curriculum/sd/`. `scripts/upload-textbooks-all.ts` uploads them raw to `catalog/textbooks/{slug}/textbook.pdf` and sets each subject's `pdf` key. **Sudan only** — no other curriculum has textbook PDFs.

### Lesson & chapter thumbnails

`catalog/lesson-covers.ts` pulls each US lesson's and chapter's cover from the source CDN, runs it through the same Sharp pipeline, and stores it per lesson and chapter. Scoped to `US` and dependent on the live CDN at seed time. **US only.**

### Videos

`catalog/videos.ts` seeds up to two **placeholder** video rows per subject pointing at S3 keys with no backing `.mp4`. Real videos are **contributed** — a teacher pastes a YouTube or Vimeo URL through the SaaS flow; nothing is uploaded. So catalog video today is placeholder rows plus externally-hosted contributed links.

### Books

`catalog/books.ts` seeds 51 catalog books whose covers are **external Open Library URLs** — not uploaded, not on our CDN.

Most seeds are upsert-safe to re-run, and image seeds re-upload to immutable keys (also safe). The exception is `content` — it has no natural unique key, so **re-running it duplicates** the generated exams, questions, materials, and assignments. ## How it works

### Loading content

Content is authored outside the database and pulled in by seeds. `catalog/us.ts` reads `scripts/us-curriculum/us-inventory.json`; `catalog/sd.ts` reads the on-disk tree under `curriculum/sd/`. Each seed upserts rows into `catalog_subjects`, `catalog_chapters`, and `catalog_lessons` and is safe to re-run. The default `catalog` seed loads US and the full Sudan and registers them; the deep curricula and the six subjects-only nationals (`sa.ts`…`jo.ts`) are separate, explicit runs — see [Completing the catalog](#completing-the-catalog) and [Seed pipeline](#seed-pipeline).

### Images

Thumbnails are not stored per subject. Each subject points at one of the shared **concepts**, and `catalog/concepts.ts` processes the source files in `public/subjects/concepts/` into S3 under `catalog/concepts/…`, served through CloudFront. At read time `image-url.ts` builds the public URL. See [Concepts](/docs/concept) and [Image pipeline](#image-pipeline).

### Connecting a school

A school never copies the catalog. When onboarding finishes, `completeOnboarding` (`src/components/onboarding/legal/actions.ts`) calls `setupCatalogForSchool` (`src/components/catalog/setup.ts`): it infers the school's curriculum from country + type, finds the matching subjects through a four-step fallback, builds the academic levels/grades/streams, and writes one `SubjectSelection` bridge row per adopted subject and grade. Those bridge rows tie a tenant to the shared content; everything school-facing — classes, timetable, library, exams — reads through them. If bridge rows go missing, `ensureSubjectSelections` repairs them lazily on first read. See [Auto-assignment](#auto-assignment).

### Reading it at runtime

Schools query the bridge, not the catalog directly — `db.subjectSelection.findMany({ where: { schoolId, gradeId }, include: { subject: { include: { chapters } } } })` returns the subjects a school adopted for a grade, joined to the shared content, carrying the optional per-school custom name and weekly-period settings. See [Query patterns](#query-patterns).

### A student learning

From a `SubjectSelection` a student gets an `Enrollment`. As lessons are watched, `LessonProgress` records the position per lesson, and when every lesson in a subject is complete a `SubjectCertificate` is issued. `syncStudentClassToEnrollment` bridges classroom membership to LMS access so a student enrolled in a class automatically gets the matching subject. See [Lesson progress and certificates](#lesson-progress-and-certificates).

### Contributing back

A teacher or school can add content — videos, materials, questions. It starts private and pending, a platform admin reviews it in the SaaS catalog (`src/components/saas-dashboard/catalog/`), and on approval it is given a visibility — private, school-only, public, or paid — and becomes part of the shared catalog for everyone. See [Content review flow](#content-review-flow).

## Why a shared catalog

| Approach           | Data for 1,000 schools × ~240 subjects                         | Sharing                                 |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Per-school clone   | 220k subjects, 800k chapters, 4M lessons                       | None                                    |
| **Shared catalog** | **~240 subjects, ~800 chapters, ~4k lessons** + bridge records | Every contribution enriches all schools |

Onboarding a school takes seconds — `setupCatalogForSchool()` reads `country` + `curriculum` and creates `SubjectSelection` bridge rows.

## Auto-assignment

`setupCatalogForSchool(schoolId, opts)` creates bridge rows via 4-step progressive fallback (the internal query is `findSubjects()`):

| Step | Filter                                 |
| ---- | -------------------------------------- |
| 1    | `country + curriculum + schoolType`    |
| 2    | `country + curriculum`                 |
| 3    | `country="*" + curriculum` (worldwide) |
| 4    | `US` (baseline fallback)               |

Grade-specific subjects produce a 1:1 mapping — "Math Grade 3" maps directly to the school's Grade 3.

```ts
import { setupCatalogForSchool } from "@/components/catalog/setup"

await setupCatalogForSchool(schoolId, { country: "US", curriculum: "US" })
await setupCatalogForSchool(schoolId, { country: "AE", curriculum: "IB-DP" })
await setupCatalogForSchool(schoolId, { country: "GB", curriculum: "GB" })
```

> The subject _selection_ works for any curriculum, but the academic _structure_ it builds (level/grade names, streams) is currently hardcoded to the Sudanese model — see [Known gaps & roadmap](#known-gaps--roadmap).

## Content review flow

```
Teacher/School creates content
  → approvalStatus: PENDING, visibility: PRIVATE
SaaS admin reviews
  → APPROVED → indexed, visibility set by creator
       PRIVATE  — only creator's school
       SCHOOL   — schools using that subject (free)
       PUBLIC   — anyone (free or paid)
  → REJECTED → feedback sent to creator
```

## Data flows

**Operational** — drives timetabling, library, exams:

```
Subject → SubjectSelection → Class → Timetable
Subject → Book → BookSelection → SchoolBook (library)
Subject → Exam → SchoolExam
Subject → Question → QuestionBank
```

**LMS** — drives e-learning + certificates:

```
Subject → Enrollment → LessonProgress → SubjectCertificate
Lesson → Video (multi-teacher)
Lesson → Attachment / Question
```

The `syncStudentClassToEnrollment()` helper (`src/lib/enrollment-sync.ts`) bridges classroom enrollment to LMS access via `Class.subjectId` → `Subject`. It's non-blocking, idempotent, and skips gracefully when the catalog FK is missing.

## Image pipeline

Server-side via Sharp, served from CloudFront CDN. Pure client/server split.

| `-md.webp`       | 600px  | 97       | Card images     |
| `-lg.webp`       | 1200px | 100      | Hero banners    |
| `-original.webp` | Full   | Lossless | Full resolution |

| File                                  | Environment | Purpose                                              |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| `src/components/catalog/image.ts`     | Server only | `processAndUploadCatalogImage`, `deleteCatalogImage` |
| `src/components/catalog/image-url.ts` | Client-safe | `getCatalogImageUrl`                                 |

`Subject` carries four S3 key fields — `thumbnail` (`@map("thumbnailKey")`), `banner` (`@map("bannerUrl")`), `cover` (textbook cover), `pdf` (textbook PDF). URL resolution priority:

1. CloudFront CDN URL from `thumbnail` → `{CDN}/{thumbnailKey}-{size}.webp`
2. Concept-based static fallback (shared concept thumbnails per [/docs/concept](/docs/concept))
3. `null` — UI falls back to color background

## Lesson progress and certificates

`LessonProgress` tracks per-lesson state: `watchedSeconds` (resume), `isCompleted`, `watchCount`, `lessonVideoId`. Linked to both `Enrollment` and `Lesson`.

When `markLessonComplete` (`src/components/stream/dashboard/lesson/catalog-actions.ts`) is called:

1. Upsert `LessonProgress` with `isCompleted: true`
2. Query all published lessons in the subject
3. If all complete → set `Enrollment.status = COMPLETED`
4. Generate `SubjectCertificate` with unique number
5. Send completion email asynchronously

## Query patterns

```ts
// All subjects for a school + grade
await db.subjectSelection.findMany({
  where: { schoolId, gradeId, isActive: true },
  include: {
    subject: {
      include: {
        chapters: {
          where: { status: "PUBLISHED" },
          orderBy: { sequenceOrder: "asc" },
        },
      },
    },
  },
})

// Lesson with ranked videos
await db.lesson.findUnique({
  where: { id: lessonId },
  include: {
    videos: {
      where: {
        approvalStatus: "APPROVED",
        OR: [{ visibility: "PUBLIC" }, { schoolId }, { isFeatured: true }],
      },
      orderBy: [
        { isFeatured: "desc" },
        { viewCount: "desc" },
        { averageRating: "desc" },
      ],
    },
  },
})

// Catalog questions for a chapter test
await db.question.findMany({
  where: {
    catalogChapterId: chapterId,
    approvalStatus: "APPROVED",
    visibility: "PUBLIC",
    status: "PUBLISHED",
  },
  orderBy: [{ qualityScore: "desc" }, { usageCount: "desc" }],
})
```

## Seed pipeline

`seedFullCatalog` (the `full` seed) runs everything in canonical order — all 12 curricula → registry → concept images. The default `catalog` seed is the fast subset: US + the full Sudan, registered. Every other curriculum ships as its own seed; per-source files only write the `curriculum` code, and `registry` owns the 12 `Curriculum` records + the `curriculumId` backfill.

| Seed (`pnpm db:seed:single …`)                    | Contents                                                                       |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| `full`                                            | `seedFullCatalog` — all 12 curricula → registry → concept images (fresh DB)    |
| `catalog`                                         | Fast default: US (~240) + full Sudan + registry                                |
| `us` / `sd`                                       | US (US-curriculum inventory) / full Sudan from `curriculum/sd/` (125 subjects) |
| `gb` / `cbse` / `caie-igcse` / `ib`               | Deep curricula via `engine.ts` (`syncCurriculumTree`) from `curriculum/<dir>/` |
| `sa` / `eg` / `ae` / `qa` / `kw` / `jo`           | The 6 subjects-only nationals — one key each (`seedSubjectsOnly`)              |
| `registry`                                        | All 12 `Curriculum` records + backfill `Subject.curriculumId`                  |
| `concepts` / `banners`                            | Concept thumbnails & banners → Sharp → S3/CloudFront (owned, shared)           |
| `books` / `content` / `videos` / `exam-templates` | Books, materials, videos, exam templates                                       |
| `demo`                                            | Academic structure + bridge rows for the demo school                           |

```bash
pnpm db:seed:single full         # everything in one run (all 12 + registry + images)
# …or piecemeal:
pnpm db:seed:single catalog      # fast default: US + full Sudan + registry
pnpm db:seed:single gb           # England — deep, from curriculum/uk
pnpm db:seed:single sa           # Saudi Arabia — subjects-only national
pnpm db:seed:single registry     # (re)link all 12 Curriculum records
pnpm db:seed:single concepts     # Thumbnails → S3/CDN
```

For exact subject/chapter/lesson counts and the seed architecture, see [Curriculum engineering](/docs/curriculum-engineering) and [Sudan curriculum](/docs/sudan-curriculum).

## Completing the catalog

The catalog is now **six curricula deep and twelve curricula wide** (see
[Curriculum coverage](#curriculum-coverage)). US and Sudan carry the most
content; England, Cambridge IGCSE, IB-DP, and Indian CBSE were brought to real
depth by the shared [tree engine](#seed-pipeline) (`syncCurriculumTree`). The six
Arab national curricula remain subjects-only. What's left is breadth, deploy
orchestration, and one resolved-but-unmerged decision.

### What shipped

- **`syncCurriculumTree`** — the generalized ingest helper that reads any
  `curriculum/<dir>/g{N}/<subject>/structure.json` tree. Replaces the
  per-curriculum copy-paste; `gb.ts`, `cbse.ts`, `caie-igcse.ts`, and `ib.ts`
  are thin callers (~20 lines each).
- **GB depth** — `gb.ts` ingests `curriculum/uk` (184 / 363 / 730), retiring the
  old subjects-only British block (then in the retired `world.ts`).
- **Authoring tooling** — `curriculum/_build_tools/engine.py` writes the trees;
  `scripts/gen-curriculum-structure.ts` turns a compact chapter-list spec into a
  `structure.json` tree; `scripts/upload-textbooks-all.ts` pushes any tree's
  textbook PDFs to S3.

### Dual US — resolved

The database US (240 / 750 / 3,993, code `US`) stays canonical;
the disk `curriculum/us` (Aldar American, 128 / 266 / 535) is **not ingested** —
it collides on the `us-g{N}-*` slug space and is shallower. It remains on disk as
a reference overlay. No merge planned.

### What remains

1. **Breadth** — the deep transnational trees (Cambridge, IB, CBSE) cover a
   representative grade; extending to every grade is mechanical (author more
   `structure.json`). The six Arab nationals need ministry textbook structures
   (Arabic sources — owner-supplied) before they graduate from `sa.ts`-style files to deep trees.
2. **Orchestration** — the default `pnpm db:seed` still loads only US + the Sudan
   base set; `gb`/`cbse`/`caie-igcse`/`ib`/`sd`/`world`/`concepts`/`banners` are
   manual single-seed runs. Fold them into one default pipeline so a fresh
   database has every curriculum and its images. (Deploy-coupled — see
   [Known gaps & roadmap](#known-gaps--roadmap).)

### Connecting concept images

Every subject in every curriculum already resolves to one of the shared concepts — the national files assign them inline; US and Sudan through `NAME_TO_CONCEPT`. What remains:

- **Chapter and lesson images** are wired only for US and Sudan, because only those have chapters and lessons. They come for free for the eight world curricula once depth lands: `us.ts` and `sd.ts` rotate chapters through a `SUBJECT_CONCEPT_POOL`, and the generalized sync inherits that.
- **One source of truth for the maps.** `NAME_TO_CONCEPT` lives in three places (`concepts.ts`, `us.ts`, and `sd.ts` as `SUBJECT_CONCEPT_MAP`) and the rotation pool in two; the US copy has already drifted (missing the Arabic keys). Hoist them into the shared core (`src/components/catalog/`) so every seed reads one map.
- **Finish the assets.** `concepts.ts` uploads only the `thumbnail` variant; `banners.ts` covers 10 of 23 concepts, so the other 13 `banner` keys resolve to missing objects, and no `cover` object is uploaded. Extend the concept seed to produce `banner` (and `cover`, if used) for all concepts, or drop the unused keys. The `arabic` source image is currently orphaned — no subject maps to it.

## Known gaps & roadmap

The content layer is now multi-curriculum with real depth for six curricula (see [Curriculum coverage](#curriculum-coverage)). The remaining work is deploy sequencing, content breadth, seed-code debt, the build, and the still-Sudanese provisioning layer.

### Deploy (load-bearing order)

The canonical codes + tree-seeded curricula are shipped in code but **not yet in the live DB**. At deploy, in order:

1. Run `prisma/migrations/20260608120000_canonicalize_curriculum_codes` (UPDATE legacy `Curriculum.code` / `Subject.curriculum` → canonical, split colliding `national` to its country, ALTER the column default).
2. Delete the stale subjects-only rows superseded by the deep trees: `gb-national-g*` (old world GB) and `ib-diploma-g*` (old world IB).
3. Re-run the catalog seeds in order: `gb`, `cbse`, `caie-igcse`, `ib`, then `concepts` / `banners`.

### Content depth

- **Six Arab nationals are subjects-only** (SA/EG/AE/QA/KW/JO — `sa.ts`…`jo.ts`). Their chapter/lesson depth needs the ministry textbook structures (Arabic TOCs), authored as `curriculum/<dir>/` trees.
- **The deep transnational curricula cover a representative grade** (Cambridge g10, IB g12 DP, CBSE g10). Extend to all grades by authoring more `structure.json` via `scripts/gen-curriculum-structure.ts`. IB lost the old PYP/MYP grade breadth when it moved to the DP tree — re-add PYP/MYP grades.
- **Encrypted / commercial textbooks** (NCERT Math, Cambridge/IB publishers) are not auto-fetched — owner-supplied.

### Seed & content debt

- **`content.ts` is not idempotent** — no business-key unique, so re-runs duplicate exams/questions/materials/assignments. Add a deterministic `seedKey @unique` per artifact.
- **Concept maps duplicated 4×** (`concepts.ts`, `us.ts`, `sd.ts`, `engine.ts`) — hoist into one shared `src/components/catalog/concepts-data.ts`.
- **Assets incomplete** — `banners.ts` skips the `arabic` concept and has a dead fallback; the `cover` S3 objects are referenced on every Subject but never uploaded.
- **Perf** — the national seeders run ~2,850 sequential awaits (findUnique + create) through `seedSubjectsOnly`; batch with `createMany`. Each national graduates to a deep `engine.ts` caller as its `curriculum/<cc>/` tree lands (GB/IB already did).
