This doc is the **competitions and hackathons inventory**. Competitions are short-cycle (1-3 months), often non-dilutive, and serve as a credibility multiplier even when we don't win. Adjacent to [Accelerators](/docs/accelerators) but distinct: shorter, cheaper to enter, and often deliberately public.

_Last verified: 2026-05-18._

## Executive summary

Why we apply to competitions:

1. **Capital** — prizes range from $5K to $1M; many winners get follow-on access.
2. **Visibility** — finalists get press, jury attention, mentor networks.
3. **Customer discovery** — judging panels include ministry buyers and corporate partners we couldn't otherwise reach.
4. **Deadline forcing function** — competitions force us to ship polish on schedule.

Why we don't apply to all:

- Time cost per application can be 20-40 hours; we cap at 6-8 competitions per year.
- Pay-to-pitch competitions are a filter (we never pay an entry fee > $50).
- Niche themes that don't map to EdTech (climate-only, blockchain-only, etc.) get filtered out.

## Selection criteria

Apply if at least **three** of these are true:

1. Prize includes non-dilutive cash or grant.
2. Judging panel includes target buyers (ministry of education officials, school owners, corporate L&D leads).
3. Open to MENA / Africa / global participants (not US-only).
4. Theme aligns: education, K-12, EdTech, AI for good, Arabic-language tech, open source, digital public infrastructure.
5. Entry fee is free or < $50.
6. Past winners include credible startups (not just "TechCrunch-Disrupt-style" hype companies).

## Application priority (rank-ordered)

1. **NTDP Innovation Challenges** — direct KSA government channel, Connect PoC up to $200K, named Vision 2030 priority.
2. **MIT Enterprise Forum Arab Startup Competition** — MENA flagship; up to $200K + accelerator pathway; finalist alone is credibility.
3. **GESS Education Innovation Awards** — direct EdTech category, exhibitor exposure to MENA school buyers.
4. **MSAR Hackathon** — Sudan-anchored, already documented in [Incubators](/docs/incubators).
5. **HolonIQ Global EdTech 1000** — nomination-based, visibility only but very high signal.
6. **WSIS Prizes** — UN platform; matches Sudan reconstruction + Arabic-first digital public infrastructure narrative.
7. **Misk Innovation Hackathons** — multiple per year, KSA exposure.
8. **LEAP Pitch Competitions** — annual Riyadh, $430M+ event ecosystem, target audience attends.

Everything else stays watching unless a specific angle opens.

## Application workflow

For each competition:

1. **Read past winners** — extract pattern; tune narrative.
2. **Identify the jury** — research 1-2 judges per panel; pre-empt their likely questions.
3. **Pick the angle** — EdTech-only events get the [Traction](/docs/traction) signal. Generalist events get the impact-first version from [Pitch](/docs/pitch) §grant pitch.
4. **Prepare the pitch deck variant** — most competitions cap at 5-10 slides. Strip [Deck](/docs/deck) to fit.
5. **Submit via T2 or T4** ([Outreach](/docs/outreach)) — competitions often have program manager intros; introduce yourself with the cold email before formal submission.
6. **Practice the pitch** — 3-min, 5-min, 10-min versions. Have all three ready.
7. **Log status** in the table above + [Data Room](/docs/data-room).
8. **Post-event** — finalist or not, follow up with every judge and 2-3 fellow finalists.

## What competitions ARE NOT

- A reliable source of capital. Most startups don't win. Plan as if you won't.
- A substitute for direct sales. Customer discovery in the panel is a side benefit, not the strategy.
- A vanity collector. We don't apply for line items on a Wikipedia page; we apply for capital, customers, or deadline forcing.
