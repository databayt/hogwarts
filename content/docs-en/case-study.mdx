A case study is proof. It is the artifact that converts the next pilot, the next paying school, and the next contributor. We aim to publish one case study per pilot — three by Q3 2026, six by Q1 2027. The first one is in progress with [King Fahad Schools](https://kingfahad.databayt.org). For pilot strategy see [Pilot](/docs/pilot); for marketing distribution see [Marketing](/docs/marketing).

## Why case studies matter

| For audience           | What the case study does                                |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Next prospective pilot | "A school like mine is doing this — I'm not the first." |
| Investors / incubators | Validates the product is in use, not in slides          |
| Contributors           | Shows the product affects real Arabic-speaking students |
| Press                  | Provides a story, a name, a face, a quote               |
| Government procurement | De-risks the decision; provides a reference call        |
| Internal team          | Reminds us why we're doing the work                     |

One paragraph from a real principal beats ten paragraphs from us.

## Anatomy of a Hogwarts case study

Every case study uses the same five-section structure. Length target: 600–900 words. Sticks to one page printed.

| #   | Section            | Purpose                                                                              | Length    |
| --- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------- |
| 1   | Headline + meta    | School, country, size, pilot start date — orient the reader instantly                | 1–2 lines |
| 2   | Problem            | What the school's day looked like before Hogwarts (concrete, with hours/percentages) | 150 words |
| 3   | What we did        | The pilot — onboarding, training, customizations, timeline                           | 200 words |
| 4   | Results            | Hours saved, parents reached, errors reduced — quantified                            | 200 words |
| 5   | Quote + closing    | A direct quote from the principal or admin; what's next                              | 100 words |
| —   | Photo / screenshot | One Arabic-UI screenshot AND one school-context photo (when permitted)               | —         |

Anti-pattern: lyrical descriptions, unverified claims, percentages without baselines, generic stock photos.

## Publication plan

Each case study lives in three places:

| Surface                                       | Format                                       | Audience                |
| --------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| `hogwarts.databayt.org/case-studies/{{slug}}` | Long-form web page, Arabic + English         | Prospects, contributors |
| PDF in `/docs/case-study/pdf/{{slug}}.pdf`    | One-page print version (sent with proposals) | Decision makers         |
| Social distribution                           | LinkedIn AR + EN, Twitter, YouTube short     | Mass audience           |

**Distribution rhythm**: tease 2 weeks before, publish, then re-share on 30/60/90 day rhythm with new angles (one number, one quote, one screenshot).

**Translations**: Arabic-first wording approved by the school; English mirror by Samia. Always credit the school in their preferred language order.

## Permissions and consent

| Asset                 | Consent required            | How captured                                                     |
| --------------------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| School name + logo    | Written, in pilot agreement | Standard pilot agreement clause                                  |
| Quote attribution     | Written, per case study     | Email confirmation; school controls final wording                |
| Numeric results       | School reviews + signs off  | Sent for review 14 days before publication                       |
| Photo / video         | Written, per asset          | Photo release form (Arabic + English)                            |
| Public reference call | Verbal, in pilot agreement  | Recorded in `customers.json`; called sparingly (3 / school / yr) |

Schools always see the draft 14 days before publication. They can request edits or veto without question — the relationship is the asset.
